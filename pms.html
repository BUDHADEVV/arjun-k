What is Portfolio Management Service (PMS)?

Understanding PMS with simple explanations and real-world analogies

Portfolio Management Service (PMS) is a professional investment solution where SEBI-registered portfolio managers create and manage a personalized portfolio of securities on behalf of high-net-worth clients. Unlike mutual funds, you directly own the underlying securities.

PMS provides customized investment solutions based on your risk appetite, financial goals, and investment horizon. The minimum investment is ₹50 lakh as mandated by SEBI, making it suitable for elite investors seeking personalized wealth management.

Simple Analogy: The Personal Designer

Imagine you want to build a luxury home. Instead of buying a ready-made apartment, you hire a renowned architect who designs a custom home based on your lifestyle, preferences, and budget requirements.

The architect selects the best materials, supervises construction, and makes adjustments as needed. You own the land and every brick of the house. The architect charges a fee for their expertise and ongoing maintenance.

Similarly with PMS:

Instead of an architect, you have a portfolio manager who creates a custom investment portfolio. You own the actual stocks and securities, not just units in a pooled fund.

Types of PMS

Choose the right PMS type based on your involvement preference and investment style

Discretionary PMS

Portfolio manager has full authority to make investment decisions within agreed guidelines without seeking prior approval for each transaction.

Control: Fund Manager

Approval: Not Required

Best For: Busy Professionals

Non-Discretionary PMS

Portfolio manager provides investment advice and recommendations, but client approval is required before executing any transactions.

Control: Shared

Approval: Client Required

Best For: Active Participants

Advisory PMS

Portfolio manager only provides investment advice and recommendations. Client makes final decisions and executes transactions independently.

Control: Client

Execution: Client

Best For: Self-Directed Investors

Who Can Invest in PMS?

As per SEBI regulations, PMS is designed for high-net-worth individuals (HNIs) and institutions with substantial investment corpus and risk-bearing capacity.

Minimum Investment

₹50 lakh per client as mandated by SEBI

Target Investors

HNIs, Ultra HNIs, NRIs, Corporates, Institutions

Investment Horizon

Typically 3-5 years for optimal results

Fee Structure

Management Fee: 1.5% - 3% per annum
Performance Fee: 10% - 20% of profits
Entry/Exit Load: Usually Nil
Other Charges: Brokerage, Taxes

Example: ₹1 crore investment may cost ₹2-3 lakh annually in management fees
